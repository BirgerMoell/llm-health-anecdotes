## Table 5. Evidence Quality Categories

| category | meaning | manuscript use |
|---|---|---|
| Raw first-person account | Patient describes their own use directly. | Best for thematic evidence when non-sensitive and source is stable. |
| Caregiver/family account | Family member describes use for another person. | Useful for pediatric, oncology, ICU, and chronic-care contexts. |
| Patient forum account | User posts in a health-specific forum. | Useful for routine patient work and community norms. |
| Media-mediated account | Journalist or institution reports patient story. | Useful but should be labeled as reported anecdote. |
| Company-mediated account | Story appears through company-affiliated source. | Use cautiously and do not count as independent replication. |
| Clinical/expert-reported case | Case appears in clinical publication or clinician report. | Useful for harm context, not equivalent to patient narrative. |
| Peer norm-setting | User advises others how to use or verify AI. | Useful for diffusion and norms, not outcome claims. |
